Reel For Reel

Reel For Reel is ultimately a project aimed at creating high-quality, low-cost, and competitive fishing reels that can either be 3D printed or sent off to a CNC service, and assembled using off-the-shelf hardware and components. This project started with the goal of making a custom reel for my dad that would be impressive as ones featured on Instagram and that I could be proud of. With high-end fishing reels from brands like Daiwa and Shimano costing upwards of $800, I see a need for a more affordable alternative that offers similar performance. This would be beneficial for anglers on a budget or those seeking a custom experience. I don't see anything morally wrong with this approach, as those with more money will still purchase from established brands, while those on tighter budgets who are willing to invest time and effort will be rewarded. Additionally, those who assemble their own reels will gain a better understanding of how they work and will be able to perform maintenance and repairs themselves.

  • Status: R&D/MODEL BEING MADE

ZtarStyle

Project ZtarStyle was created because parts in the e-moto modding scene are very expensive and some are designed in styles that don’t suit every build. I plan to design parts that are sleek, modern, and durable, which will suit a wide range of builds, from full-on supermoto/stunt bikes to cafe racers and ultimate daily machines.

  • Status: Waiting for delivery of the Ventus One so I can take accurate measurements and have a test bed (Ventus One is using 79Bikes Falcon frame which is a derivative of the Surron frame)
